What are Quads in Poker?

In poker, 'Quads' refer to a hand containing four cards of the same rank, regardless of their suit. This hand is also known as 'Four of a Kind'. For example, four Queens or four 7s would be Quads.

Understanding Quads

Quads is one of the highest-ranking hands in poker, only surpassed by a Straight Flush and a Royal Flush. The probability of being dealt Quads in a game like Texas Hold'em is quite low, making it a rare and powerful hand.

Playing Quads

When you have Quads, you typically have a very strong hand that is likely to win at showdown. The main strategy when playing Quads is figuring out how to maximize the pot and get as many chips from your opponents as possible.

Example of "Quads"

For instance, if you're playing a hand of Texas Hold'em and you're dealt two 9s, and the flop comes out 9-9-A, you have Quads.

Qualifier

In High-Low games, it is a requirement the Low hand must meet to win the pot.

 

Quart

A four-card straight flush.

 

Quartered

To divide half a pot between two tying hands in split pot games. This is Applicable to Omaha Hi-Lo Poker.

 

Quint

A straight flush.

 

Quint Major

A royal straight flush.

 

Quitting Time

An agreed-upon time to end a poker game (Curfew).

 

Quorum

The minimum number of players needed to start a poker game.
